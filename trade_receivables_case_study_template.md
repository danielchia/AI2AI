### 4. Final Decision & Terms

**Recommendation:** **APPROVE** with pricing adjustment
- Advance: **85%** (vs. 90% standard) due to unrated buyer
- Holdback: **15%** (released post-payment)
- Discount Rate: **5.5% per annum** (vs. 5% standard) – premium for unrated buyer
- Effective Funding: USD 425,000 (85% of USD 500K)
- Holdback Held: USD 75,000 (released when buyer pays)

**Rationale:**
- Strong invoice quality, perfect payment history, robust documentation offset unrated buyer status
- 85% advance and 5.5% discount appropriate risk-adjusted terms
- Monitor buyer credit closely; if payment received on-time, future invoices from this buyer eligible for standard 90% advance

### 5. Key Insights & Lessons Learned

#### What Worked Well
1. **Robust KYC & buyer confirmation:** Early buyer confirmation (within 48 hours) de-risks payment disputes
2. **Clear documentation:** BoL + delivery receipt + signed invoice = indisputable proof of performance
3. **Long payment history:** 18-month, 100% on-time history with same buyer = confidence in repayment
4. **Non-recourse structure:** Seller insolvency does not affect Purchaser's claim on receivable
5. **Singaporean framework:** MAS regulation + SIAC arbitration provides legal clarity

#### What Needed Careful Attention
1. **Unrated buyer credit:** Required pricing adjustment; traditional banks would demand guarantees
2. **Commodity price volatility:** Chemical markets can shift quickly; 90-day tenor captures some price risk
3. **Single buyer concentration:** Even one strong buyer at 2-3% can become material; portfolio limits essential
4. **Cross-border settlement:** Instructions to buyer to pay Purchaser required clear notice and wire account verification

#### Process Improvements for Future Deals
- Develop seller financial metric tracking (quarterly P&L updates)
- Implement buyer payment performance dashboard (on-time %, days-past-due average)
- Create tiered advance schedules based on buyer credit rating or payment history
- Formalize quarterly portfolio review to monitor concentration creep

The 24 forcing questions (8 each: Process, Legal, Credit) are designed to surface these exact issues **before** funding. By requiring answers to all 24 questions and achieving 70%+ pass rate per agent, the system prevents:
- Funding receivables without buyer confirmation (P2)
- Accepting unrated buyers without documentation (C1)
- Overlooking title defects or prior pledges (P7)
- Proceeding without perfected security interests (L3)
