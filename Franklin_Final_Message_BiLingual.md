## ENGLISH

Hey Franklin 👋

Now that the LinkLogis deal is almost done, I've been thinking about how to make your capital sourcing smoother.

Onboarding Wilson and Chemtank was tough: lots of meetings, back-and-forth between traders and capital teams. It got me thinking: what if traders could prep pitches against investor mandates before the manual back-and-forth starts?

The insight came from Garry Tan (President/CEO of Y Combinator).

He released Gstack—a Claude Code framework that applies forcing questions to pressure-test ideas before engineering time is wasted. At YC office hours, ~1 in 3 projects get abandoned because they don't survive scrutiny. He basically used AI to create a structure to allow startups' AI to communicate and improve pitches (AI2AI).

I realized the same methodology works for investor mandates. So I built a working pilot for trade receivables this week:

3 AI agents (Process, Legal, Compliance) evaluate deals against investor mandates using forcing questions. Instead of vague criteria ("good fit"), every decision is transparent + repeatable. Mandates become JSON schemas. Settlement structure become infrastructure requirements. Deals get evaluated in ~3 seconds instead of 2-4 weeks.

The beauty: traders can use AI to adapt their pitches to investor requirements before submitting. LinkLogis can keep all sensitive info (cost of capital, internal hierarchy, contracts) completely separate.

**Why LinkLogis is perfect for this:**
- You're already doing institutional capital deployment in trade receivables
- You have real investor mandates (not theoretical)
- You can test: does this make it easier for traders to self-prep using AI?

**4-week pilot:**
1. Light MOU
2. We create a deal proposal through the system
3. Proposal evaluated by 3 agents (process ✓ legal ✓ compliance ✓)
4. Your team gives feedback on decision quality vs. your manual experience
5. Help refine the forcing questions

This could grow into something bigger—a mandate AI2AI play for the entire institutional capital market. Worth a conversation?

## 中文版本

嗨Franklin 👋

LinkLogis的融资快要搞定了，我一直在想怎样能让你们的融资流程更顺畅。

当时Wilson和Chemtank的融资最麻烦的地方，就是要开很多会，交易员和融资团队之间沟通特别多。这让我在想：如果交易员能在手工对接之前，就根据投资者的mandate来准备pitch，会不会更高效？

这个想法来自Garry Tan（Y Combinator的总裁兼CEO）。

他开源了Gstack—一个Claude Code框架，用forcing questions（强制性问题）来pressure-test创意，防止浪费工程时间。在YC的office hours里，大约1/3的项目因为经不起这些问题的审视就被放弃了。他基本上是用AI来创建一个结构，让初创公司的AI能够相互沟通和改进pitch（AI2AI）。

我意识到这个methodology也适用于投资者的mandate。所以这周我就快速为应收账款融资构建了一个working pilot：

3个AI agents（流程、法律、合规）用forcing questions来评估deals是否符合投资者mandates。与其用模糊的标准（"fit不fit"），每个决策都是透明的、可重复的。Mandates变成JSON schemas。结算结构变成基础设施要求。Deals的评估从2-4周降到~3秒。

最妙的是：交易员可以用AI在提交之前就根据投资者要求调整pitch。LinkLogis所有敏感信息（融资成本、内部架构、合同）完全独立保存。

**为什么LinkLogis最适合做这个试点：**
- 你们已经在做机构投资者的应收账款融资
- 你们有真实的投资者mandates（不是理论的）
- 你们可以测试：这套系统能不能帮交易员更好地用AI自我调整？

**4周的试点计划：**
1. 签简单的MOU
2. 我们通过系统创建一个deal proposal
3. Proposal被3个agents评估（流程✓ 法律✓ 合规✓）
4. 你的团队反馈评估质量 vs 手工对接的体验
5. 帮我们优化forcing questions

这有可能发展成更大的东西—整个机构资本市场的mandate AI2AI。有兴趣讨论一下吗？
